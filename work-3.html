Flow Packaging Machine

This project focused on the mechanical design of a flow packaging machine for dry baked goods, such as muffins or croissants. Developed as part of a machine design course, the goal was to automate product alignment, feeding, and sealing.

A chain-and-pin mechanism was created to ensure regular spacing between products. Sprockets drive a roller chain fitted with polyethylene guides, chosen for their low friction and high wear resistance. These guides play a key role in maintaining precise positioning throughout the packaging process.

Following the professor‚Äôs recommendation, the team chose not to include the plastic film feed module in this phase of development, allowing focus on the core transport and sealing operations.

The thermal sealing unit is powered by a geared motor connected to the drive rollers via a chain transmission. It applies controlled heat and pressure to the base of the packaging film, ensuring a consistent seal.

The design emphasizes mechanical rigidity, ease of maintenance, and effective thermal isolation. All components were dimensioned to handle various product dimensions while minimizing vibration and ensuring seal quality.

The final assembly brings together all functional subsystems into a streamlined packaging line. From product loading to bottom sealing, the system ensures continuous flow, structural integrity, and operational safety throughout the process.

This final development phase highlighted key mechanical engineering practices, including chain transmission alignment, structural integration, and precision assembly. It strengthened my competencies in mechanical systems design, while enhancing my ability to navigate real-world engineering challenges with technical accuracy and problem-solving rigor.
